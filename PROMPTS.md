# Sports

## 1. Campus World Cup Watch Party Finder

**Problem:** Students want to watch big games (like the World Cup) together but don't know where events are happening on campus.

**Scenario:** Build a simple app or website where students can create, find, and join watch parties. Include features like game schedules, RSVP lists, and locations on campus. Keep it lightweight and social.

## 2. Student-Athlete Weekly Planner

**Problem:** Student athletes struggle to keep track of practices, classes, and assignments in one place.

**Scenario:** Build a simple planner that combines academic and athletic schedules. You could include calendar views, reminders, and quick task tracking--nothing overly complex, just something useful and usable fast.

## 3. Pickup Game Matchmaker

**Problem:** Students want to play sports casually but don't know who's available or where games are happening.

**Scenario:** Design a tool that lets users quickly post or join pickup games (soccer, basketball, etc.). Include time, location, and number of players needed. Keep it fast--like "post a game in 10 seconds."

## 4. Fairness and Integrity in Sports

**Problem:** Mistakes in refereeing or unclear rules can affect game outcomes.

**Scenario:** Come up with a project that supports fairness-like reviewing plays, teaching rules, or helping fans understand decisions better. This could use video, diagrams, simulations, or even a board game.

**Scenario:** Build a tool that helps students easily discover and use campus resources. Your solution could include a centralized platform, smart search, or personalized recommendations to connect students with the right services at the right time.

## 3. Optimizing Campus Space Usage

**Problem:** Universities often lack clear insight into how spaces, labs, and shared technology are used and overlooking their revenue generating.

**Scenario:** Create a tool that helps track and visualize how campus spaces are utilized. Your solution could include booking systems, usage analytics, or real-time availability dashboards to improve efficiency, access, and revenue generation.

**Problem:** Students who are struggling academically or personally are often identified too late.

**Scenario:** Build a system that helps identify early warning signs and connect students to support services proactively. You could use data inputs, check-ins, or alert systems to guide timely intervention and support.

# Health & Bioinformatics

## 1. Improving Nutrition Awareness

**Problem:** Many people struggle to understand what they're eating and how it affects their health.

**Scenario:** Build a tool that helps users make better food choices by analyzing meals or providing simple nutritional insights. You could include barcode scanning, meal suggestions, or personalized feedback based on goals or dietary needs.

## 2. Enhancing Patient-Doctor Communication

**Problem:** Patients often forget symptoms, questions, or details when visiting a doctor.

**Scenario:** Create a solution that helps patients track symptoms, prepare for appointments, and communicate more effectively with healthcare providers. This could include summaries, question prompts, or shareable reports.

## 3. Early Detection of Health Risks

**Problem:** Many health conditions go unnoticed until they become serious. You are not diagnosing, you are providing patients with resources.

**Scenario:** Design a tool that helps identify potential health risks early based on user input, lifestyle habits, or basic screening questions. Focus on clear feedback and encouraging preventive care.

## 4. Managing Chronic Conditions

**Problem:** People with chronic illnesses often need help staying on top of treatments and lifestyle changes.

**Scenario:** Build a system that helps users manage medications, track symptoms, and stay consistent with care plans. You could include reminders, progress tracking, or motivational features to support long-term health.

# Finance

## 1. Simplifying Student Loan Management

**Problem:** Many people struggle to understand and manage their student loans.

**Scenario:** Build a tool that helps users track their loans, estimate repayment timelines, and explore different payment strategies. You could include visualizations, reminders, or "what-if" scenarios for different repayment plans.

## 2. Smarter Subscription Management

**Problem:** People often lose track of subscriptions and recurring payments.

**Scenario:** Design a tool that helps users identify, track, and manage subscriptions. Your solution could include alerts for upcoming charges, suggestions to cancel unused services, or a simple dashboard of monthly costs.

## 3. Improving Financial Literacy for Beginners

**Problem:** Many people lack basic financial knowledge like credit scores, interest, or investing fundamentals, but could be serviced by visualizing the goals they are aiming for.

**Scenario:** Create an interactive experience that teaches core financial concepts in a simple and engaging way. This could be a game, quiz-based app, or visual guide tailored to beginners.

## 4. Emergency Fund Planning

**Problem:** People often don't have a clear plan for saving for emergencies.

**Scenario:** Build a tool that helps users set realistic savings goals, track progress, and stay motivated. You could include personalized plans, milestone tracking, or tips based on income and expenses.
